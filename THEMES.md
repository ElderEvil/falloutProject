# UI Themes (User Preference)

The game supports **multiple visual themes** that affect the entire interface.
Themes are **purely cosmetic** and do **not** impact gameplay, balance, or
progression.

Only **one theme** can be active at a time.

---

## Fallout 3 — Classic Green Terminal

**Style:** Green monochrome terminal
**Mood:** Cold, utilitarian, institutional

- High‑contrast green on black
- Strong glow and sharp borders
- Closest to classic Fallout terminals
- Most readable and accessible

**Recommended for:**
Players who prefer the original Pip‑Boy look.

---

## Fallout: New Vegas — Amber Terminal

**Style:** Amber / yellow terminal
**Mood:** Dusty, analog, worn

- Warm amber tones
- Slightly softer contrast
- Feels older and more “human”
- Evokes Mojave‑era terminals

**Recommended for:**
Players who want a warmer, narrative‑focused aesthetic.

---

## Fallout 4 — Modern Teal Terminal

**Style:** Teal / cyan terminal
**Mood:** Clean, modern, rebuilt

- Cool teal palette
- Minimal visual noise
- Feels newer and more refined
- Comfortable for long sessions

**Recommended for:**
Players who prefer a modern UI look.

---

## Radiation / AI Accent (Overlay)

**Purpose:** Visual distinction for AI‑generated or unstable content

- Uses the active theme’s primary color
- Subtle glow or pulse on appearance
- Fades quickly to normal

**Applied to:**
AI‑generated text, system alerts, hazardous events.

---

## Theme Rules

- Themes affect **colors only**
- Layout, animations, and behavior remain unchanged
- Themes can be switched at any time
- User preference is persisted locally
